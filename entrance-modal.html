Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS

◈ Hyperbolic Arrival

3I/ATLAS is an interstellar traveler — an object not bound to our Sun, moving on a fully hyperbolic orbit. It enters our system only once, slices through space, and leaves forever. Its trajectory carries the fingerprints of a distant star system we may never see.

◈ Solar Perihelion & Micro-Vector Shift

At perihelion — its closest sweep past the Sun — gravitational forces peak. Even a minuscule velocity change here, on the order of a few meters per second, becomes a massive long-distance course correction. This is the same gravitational leverage spacecraft use during planetary flybys to bend their path across the solar system.

◈ Jupiter's Hill Radius

Jupiter's Hill sphere is the region (~0.355 AU, ~53 million km) where its gravity dominates over the Sun's pull. When your HUD displays ⚠ INSIDE HILL SPHERE, it means the object has crossed into Jupiter's gravitational domain — a zone where deflection, perturbation, or even temporary capture becomes possible.
